About Us

Grace Club is an Investment Club created by Opes Investment Firm and four other individuals to achieve the goals of learning, saving, and investing in a group setting.

The idea is to introduce members to saving and investing, assist them as they open their bank accounts and SCD accounts, and assist them in gaining confidence to save and invest on their own by pointing them to credible sources of information and professional investment institutions. By doing this, we aim to cover the gap between ordinary Ugandans and financial institutions, thus fostering equitable financial inclusion.

Grace club is currently the only investment club of its kind in Uganda. Established in March 2022, it is a member-driven venture that strives to create a community of members with knowledge and a network for saving and investing.

By engaging like-minded people in collaboration with professionals, the club aims to provide a forum where the highest standard of learning how to save and invest is fostered, developed, and applied in real life. Our group learning environment encourages informed decision-making while promoting the individual growth of tomorrow's business and society leaders.

Membership

New members are welcomed into Grace Club on the recommendation of an existing member. The maximum number of members now is set at 20. Members are involved in the management of the club.

Q&A

Do I need To be an Expert?

“Living is learning.” There is no prerequisite knowledge. We encourage the involvement of anyone with a genuine interest in learning, saving, and investing.

Why investing?

Investing involves a range of fundamental skills that extend across multiple disciplines. Investments provide a lens through which we can better understand businesses, markets, finance, innovations, and the overall improvement of life and society. It is even fun when it’s done in a group such as Grace Club.

What is the Investment Policy?

Our approach to investing is defensive. We start with investing in less risky assets like Government Bonds and stocks of dividend-paying companies in Uganda and the East African Region before venturing into other asset classes or businesses. Each member is welcome to bring to the Club a well-researched idea of investing in an agreed-upon asset class for discussion.

How will losses and profits be dealt with?

Profits or losses after all expenses are shared according to the percentage of each member's total ownership/contributions. Returns are shared twice a year.

What are the fees?

Grace Club has created a dynamic fee structure that favors members and the Club to meet its expenses. Fees are set at 2% on deposits, 2% on profits, and 10,000/= for each withdrawal. Fees assist in paying for rent, transport, communication, and other expense. Expenses that exceed the fees collected are duly communicated to the members

How much do I need to start?

The minimum required amount to become a member is 100,000/=.

Our level and ability to save differ from person to person and from time to time. With this in mind, our approach toward member contributions is individualization. After becoming a member, you contribute as much as you can. We only encourage you to contribute consistently at least 50,000/= per month.
